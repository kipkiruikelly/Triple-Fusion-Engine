What BullLogic Is

Nature of the Service
BullLogic is a market analysis and signals platform. It applies machine-learning models and ICT-style market structure analysis to publicly available market data and presents the output as predictions, charts, and alerts. BullLogic is not a broker-dealer, investment adviser, exchange, custodian, or payment institution, and is not licensed by the Capital Markets Authority of Kenya or any other financial regulator.
Trading Execution & Custody
BullLogic never holds, transfers, or has access to your trading funds or securities. Live orders placed through the platform are sent directly to your own connected MetaTrader 5 brokerage account and executed by that broker, under that broker's terms. "Paper trading" and the admin paper-trading engine simulate orders against real market prices using no real money; simulated results are always labeled as such and are never mixed with real account performance.
Model & Data Limitations
Predictions are generated by statistical and machine-learning models trained on historical data and are frequently wrong. Backtests and any published track record are hypothetical or based on past data and do not guarantee future results. Market data is sourced from third parties (including yfinance, Finnhub, and Pyth) and may be delayed, incomplete, or temporarily unavailable; where sources disagree beyond a normal tolerance, BullLogic shows both values rather than choosing one silently.

Risk Warning

Not Financial Advice
Nothing on BullLogic is investment advice, a recommendation, or an offer to buy or sell any security, currency, commodity, or digital asset. You alone are responsible for your trading decisions. Trading involves substantial risk of loss, including the possibility of losing more than your initial investment, and you should never trade money you cannot afford to lose.
Automated & Algorithmic Trading Risk
Features that place or manage live orders automatically (including trailing stop-loss automation and alert-triggered signals) act on model output without human review at the moment of execution. Connectivity issues, broker-side rejections, slippage, and model error can all result in outcomes different from what was displayed on screen. You are responsible for monitoring any automated trading you enable.
