How a trade happens

  • Signals. Two strategies run side by side. ml_ensemble takes the same LR + RF model vote shown in the app, gated by a minimum confidence. alpha_rules combines a library of quantitative alphas (momentum, mean reversion, volatility, volume) into a composite score, ranks all tracked tickers cross-sectionally, and downweights signals when the Pyth oracle confidence interval is unusually wide.
  • Entry. The engine records the real market price and its source at signal time, then fills the virtual order at that price plus the spread/slippage estimate. Commission is charged per side.
  • Sizing. Position size = (virtual equity x risk % per trade) / (ATR x stop multiple), so hitting the stop loses a fixed fraction of equity.
  • Exits. A position closes on: stop hit, target hit, opposite signal (reversal), or the max holding period elapsing. Exits pay the same friction.
  • Risk limits. Max concurrent positions per strategy, a per-asset-class exposure cap, and a daily loss circuit breaker that pauses new entries for the rest of the day. Exits always keep running.
  • Market hours. Crypto trades 24/7. US equities and ETFs only during the regular session (13:30 to 20:00 UTC, weekdays; exchange holidays are not modeled). Forex and commodities Sunday 22:00 UTC through Friday 21:00 UTC.
  • Honesty. Trades are append-only with a full audit trail. Nothing is backfilled, edited or seeded. Below the minimum closed-trade count, metrics say "insufficient data" instead of showing unstable numbers.
